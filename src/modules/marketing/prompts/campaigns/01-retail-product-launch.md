## Why this passes the gut-check

| Check | Pass | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Subject + preheader complement | ✓ | Subject is the hook ("Sunday Drive in Honey"), preheader is the choice ("Honey is the one we'd buy first"). Different words, related ideas. |
| Headline screenshot-worthy | ✓ | "Made for the long way home" reads like a friend's text, not a stock caption. |
| Section A reader-is-hero | ✓ | "Made for the friend who…" puts the reader in the scene. No "we made this." |
| Section B has specific moment | ✓ | "the 405 at 6pm," "the airport pickup at 11pm," "brunches where you don't want the glasses to be the loudest thing." All specific. |
| No banned words | ✓ | No "curated," "elevate," "premium," "must-have," "drop" (used naturally — "came back" not "dropped"). |
| Pronoun ratio | ✓ | "You/your/the friend who" outnumber "we/our" 7:1. |
| Wholesale-only checks | n/a | Retail campaign. |

## What the agency would have written (for comparison)

A reasonable $3K/mo agency, given the same brief:

> Subject: New Sunday Drive colorways — Honey, Midnight, Crystal
> Preheader: Our bestselling frame in three new shades
> Hero: Introducing Honey
> Subtitle: A curated palette for warm-weather styling.
> CTA: Shop Now
> Section A: We're so excited to launch our latest Sunday Drive
>   colorways. Designed in our LA studio, each frame elevates
>   your everyday look with timeless appeal.
> Section B: Premium UV400 protection meets effortless style.
>   Available now while supplies last.

That's not bad — it's grammatically clean, brand-name correct,
moves the right info. But:
- "Introducing," "curated," "elevates," "effortless," "premium,"
  "while supplies last" — six banned phrases in seven sentences
- Zero specificity ("warm-weather styling" vs "the 405 at 6pm")
- Customer is invisible (subject is "we")
- Headlines could appear on Sunski, Le Specs, Quay verbatim

The Jaxy v5 output is the same brief, written like a friend who
knows the customer drives down the 405 at 6pm.
