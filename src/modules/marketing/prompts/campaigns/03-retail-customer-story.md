## Why the image_75_solid variant fits here

UGC photos rarely have a top-30%-calm safe zone — they're real
moments shot by real people. Forcing the photo into a
`full_bleed_overlay` would either crop Sara's head off or require
HTML text on top of her face. The `image_75_solid` variant centers
the photo with cream gutters and puts the headline ABOVE the image
in HTML — preserves the photo's integrity and respects that Sara's
the visual hero.

The secondary image (centered_75 variant) is a clean product shot
of the Main Character in tortoise on a warm linen surface — the
"about this frame" beat that gives the email a clear product
anchor without distracting from the customer moment.

## Notes on permission + tone

The "(Photo posted with permission. Sister: write back, we'll
send you a pair.)" is the kind of fourth-wall break that the
brand bible's voice section explicitly endorses ("sideways, not
announced," "the friend in the group chat"). It also doubles as:

1. **Legal cover** — explicit permission disclosure.
2. **Engagement bait** — invites the sister (or anyone reading)
   to write back. Replies = warm leads.
3. **Brand differentiation** — competitors don't write this way.
   The line is unmistakably Jaxy.

The agency version would either omit this entirely or do it as a
formal "About Sara" sidebar. The conversational footnote is more
Jaxy.
