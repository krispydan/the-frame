## Why this passes the wholesale gut-check

| Check | Pass | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sounds like Christina, not a brand blast | ✓ | First-person "I'll put together," signed "— Christina." "I do this all day" is a signature Christina phrase. |
| Clearly human, not a bot | ✓ | Specific accounts ("Austin and Asheville"), specific reorder window ("within 10 days"), specific named frames ("Long Weekend," "Encore in Honey"). Bots write "popular new styles." |
| Offer to help beyond the ask | ✓ | "If you tell me about your store, I'll put together a six-frame starter mix" — the advisory close is the differentiator. |
| Has specific numbers | ✓ | 6 frames, 2 reordering, 10 days, $8 wholesale, $25-30 retail, $150 MOQ, 2-3 day ship. Buyers anchor on numbers — this email has 7. |
| Buyer is the hero | ✓ | "Your store," "your order," "your customer." We doesn't appear. |
| Works for $200 mom-and-pop AND $5K big-box | ✓ | Numbers + named accounts + helpful close land for either tier. The starter-mix offer specifically lowers the bar for first-time small buyers. |

## Why the grid_2up secondary fits

Six new frames is too many to feature individually — would dilute
the message. The grid_2up variant shows TWO frames (the two that
are already reordering — Long Weekend + Encore Honey) as proof
points. The other four exist in the "view the line" link. Editing
the hero down to two specific frames + a number ("six just landed")
is the same compression technique wholesale buyers expect from a
seasoned rep.

## What got cut

**Round 1 had:** *"Excited to share our six new arrivals! These
on-trend frames are perfect for any boutique looking to refresh
their summer assortment."*

- Banned: "Excited to share" (banned opener)
- Banned: "perfect for any boutique" (the "your customers will love"
  variant — assumes the buyer's customers)
- Generic: "any boutique" (no number, no specific store type)
- Wrong voice register: too DTC ("excited to share")

The v5 rewrite leads with NUMBERS (6 + 2) and named stores
(Austin, Asheville) instead of adjectives.

**Round 3 had:** *"Hey babe, six new frames in — and 2 are
already moving!"*

- Wrong register: "babe" is DTC voice, not wholesale.
- Wholesale stays professional-warm. The current opener
  ("6 new frames in — and 2 are already moving") still has
  the casual em-dash structure but loses the DTC vocative.
