## 1. Brand Foundation

### Mission
Jaxy exists to make stylish eyewear accessible to everyone. We're solving the problem of having to choose between fashion-forward design and financial practicality. People shouldn't have to spend hundreds of dollars on glasses that inevitably get scratched, lost, or damaged—nor should they settle for boring, generic designs just because they're affordable.

### Vision
In 5-10 years, Jaxy will be a recognized name in accessible eyewear with a strong community of customers who own multiple pairs and see Jaxy as part of their personal style identity. We'll be sold in hundreds of boutiques across the US, have a thriving DTC presence, and be known for distinctive designs that people genuinely love wearing.

### External Values
1. **Authentic Self-Expression** — Style should reflect who you are, not what brands tell you to be
2. **Guilt-Free Quality** — Great design and quality shouldn't come with financial anxiety
3. **Distinctive Over Generic** — We celebrate personality and refuse to make boring products
4. **Accessible Confidence** — Everyone deserves to feel good in what they wear
5. **Real Life Ready** — Our products fit into how people actually live, not aspirational fantasies

### Internal Values
1. High margins are non-negotiable
2. Wholesale at low cost so retailers always make money
3. Amazing customer relations — relationships with wholesale accounts are key
4. Be authentic - don't be too salesey. We are selling a visions, an aethetic and not a product. 

### Brand Name Usage
- **Always use "Jaxy"** — not "Jaxy Eyewear"
- In SEO titles: "| Jaxy" (not "| Jaxy Eyewear")
- The brand name is just **Jaxy**, keep it short and clean

### Voice Anchor (the one phrase repeated everywhere)
**"More frames. Less shame."**

The single phrase that carries the brand. Lives on the IG bio, the inside of every case, the back of every hangtag, every email footer, every receipt. Repetition is how this becomes ours — say it everywhere.

## 2. Target Customer (ICP)

### Retail Customer

**Demographics:**
- **Age:** 18-45 (Gen Z and younger millennials)
- **Gender:** Primarily women, but unisex appeal
- **Income:** Budget-conscious, values style over luxury spending. Aspirational.
- **Location:** US-based, urban and suburban

**Psychographics:**
- Style-conscious but practical, social media native, values authenticity over prestige
- Tired of choosing between expensive designer glasses and boring cheap options
- Wants to own multiple pairs for different moods/outfits
- Shops at Zara, H&M, Target style section, Amazon, boutique shops
- Lives on Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest

**Buying Behavior:**
- Budget-conscious but willing to pay for distinctive design
- Triggered by seeing someone wearing cool glasses, outfit completion, impulse buys
- Decides on design uniqueness, price point, how it makes them feel

### Wholesale Customer
- Independent gift shops, bookstores, vintage stroes, lifestyle boutiques looking for well-priced inventory that moves quickly and fits their aesthetic

### Anti-Customer
- People seeking luxury status symbols or designer logos
- Those who view eyewear as a years-long investment piece
- Customers needing prescription optical frames
- Anyone looking for technical/performance eyewear
- People looking for sporty eyewear

## 4. Visual Identity

### Brand Aesthetic
**1970s California retro-modern.** Sun-drenched, warm, nostalgic, bold. Think: vintage cars on Pacific Coast Highway, palm trees against blue skies, film-grain photography, groovy typography. Confident and playful yet premium-feeling.

### Logo

The Jaxy logo is a **custom wordmark** set in the Glitz typeface.

**Two versions (V2):**
- **Primary logo** — the full `Jaxy` wordmark. Use wherever the brand needs to be clearly recognized.
- **Secondary version** — the `x` icon (swash mark). Use where the name is already present and just needs reinforcement: app icons, social avatars, repeated mentions.

**Characteristics:**
- Retro-groovy bold serif/display typeface (1970s inspired, similar energy to Cooper Black)
- Capital J with distinctive curling swash descender
- Single-story lowercase 'a' for vintage feel
- Sweeping 'y' tail that curls to the right
- Tightly kerned, compact presence
- Slight organic baseline rhythm — playful, not rigid

**Logo Color Variants (V2):** Use the **light** logo on dark/saturated backgrounds and the **dark** (Espresso `#39341F`) logo on light/neutral backgrounds. Approved background colors are the eight V2 brand colors — see `BRAND-GUIDELINES.md`.

### Design Motifs

#### Organic Blob Shapes
A signature Jaxy design element: **abstract organic shapes** that evoke eyeglass lenses, butterfly wings, or smooth river stones.

**Characteristics:**
- Irregular, rounded forms — never perfectly geometric
- Typically appear in pairs (evoking two lenses of sunglasses)
- Used as background accent shapes, photo masks, or graphic elements
- Filled with V2 brand colors (e.g. Lavender + Plum, Sage + Moss) with fine grain texture overlay
- Can overlap for layered depth

**Usage:**
- Key visuals and hero sections
- Social media graphics
- Packaging accents
- Website section backgrounds
- Never more than 2-3 blobs per composition

#### Film Grain Texture
A subtle noise/grain overlay that gives graphics a tactile, analog, vintage feel.
- Apply at 5-15% opacity over solid color fills and graphic shapes
- Do NOT apply over photography (photos have their own film-grain treatment)
- Reinforces the retro aesthetic without looking damaged or dirty

## 5. Brand Voice

### 5.1 The Voice in One Line
**Jaxy is the friend on your group thread who texts back in 30 seconds with "no babe — the other one." Generous with taste, unbothered about price, rooting for you to look like yourself on purpose.**

### 5.2 The Persona — Who Jaxy *Is*

Imagine Jaxy as a person, not a brand:

She's 27, lives in Echo Park, works adjacent to fashion (a buyer at a boutique, a stylist's assistant, a freelancer who does "a little bit of everything"). Her apartment has Trader Joe's flowers in a thrifted vase, a stack of vintage *Vogue* on the coffee table, and a closet that's mostly other people's hand-me-downs styled into something better. She drives a 2008 Camry and isn't embarrassed about it.

She's the friend you send fitting-room photos to. She replies before you can put your phone down. Her reply is always either "OBSESSED" or "no the other one" — never silence, never "they're cute!" with the tone that means they're not cute. She tells you the truth because she likes you.

She doesn't gatekeep. She'll tell you where she got the dress, who cuts her hair, which farmers' market has the good bread. She has opinions but offers them like a friend, not a critic. When you're trying something on that doesn't work, she doesn't say "no." She says, *"try the other one — that one's more you."*

That's the voice. Every line of Jaxy copy is a text from her.

### 5.3 What Jaxy Believes (the Worldview)

This is the spine of the voice. Every piece of copy should ladder back to one of these:

1. **Style is for everyone — not just people with money or "an eye."** If you've ever been told you "can't pull something off," that was a lie someone sold you.
2. **Own more than one pair.** You're not the same person every day; your glasses shouldn't be either. Collect them like records.
3. **Designer doesn't equal good. Cheap doesn't equal bad. The eye does the work.** Most expensive things are not better. Most cheap things are not worse. The trick is knowing which is which.
4. **The best compliment isn't "where did you get those." It's "those are SO you."** We're not making it-bag eyewear. We're making frames that disappear into your personality and amplify it.
5. **Confidence isn't loud — it's just deciding you're done apologizing.** For your face, your taste, your budget, your moods.
6. **LA isn't aesthetic; it's a way of moving.** Sun-warmed, unhurried, a little romantic, never trying. We're from there. It's in everything we make. We don't need to keep saying it.

### 5.4 Personality Traits (Rewritten with Specifics)

These replace generic descriptors. When in doubt about a line, ask: *"Is this trait showing up?"*

1. **Generous, not gatekeepy.** Jaxy roots for everyone. Hype-girl energy without the performance. We tell you what looks good on you, what doesn't, what's worth buying two of, and where to wear it. We don't pretend things are exclusive when they're not.
2. **Best-friend honest.** We tell you the truth, but the truth comes with a hand on your shoulder. We don't say "this isn't for you." We say "try this one — it's more you." Direct. Warm. Useful.
3. **Culturally plugged in, never trying.** We know what's happening in music, fashion, internet, and LA right now. We reference it the way friends do — sideways, not announced. We don't say "we're so Brat summer." We just sound like the friend who already moved on from that joke a week ago.
4. **Playfully unbothered.** We're not anxious about our prices, our scale, or what designers are doing. We have a wink in our pocket but we don't deploy it on every line. Some moments call for the wink. Most call for the warm.
5. **Specific over generic, every time.** "Golden hour on the 10 freeway" beats "summer." "The friend who says yes to dessert" beats "for fun lovers." We sound like Jaxy because we say the things only Jaxy would say.

### 5.5 Signature Vocabulary

A short list of words and phrases that sound like Jaxy. Use them where they fit; don't shoehorn. The list is alive — add when something new lands.

**Words we use on purpose:**
- *Babe, friend, you* — direct address, sparingly. Best in email, social, customer service. Never on product pages.
- *Obsessed* — actual feeling word. Reserve for things we mean it about.
- *Made for [the person/moment]* — "Made for the friend who says yes." "Made for the 405 at 6pm." Specificity engine.
- *Two of these, please* — the permission-to-buy-multiples line. Use sparingly so it stays funny.
- *Real ones / the ones who get it* — community language. Use in social, email, never on product.
- *Try the other one* — best-friend correction without the bite.
- *Honestly* — used like a friend uses it, to soften a confident take. *"Honestly, just get them in both."*
- *On purpose* — anti-accident. "Look like yourself on purpose." "Frames you wear on purpose."

**Words we don't say (ever):**
- *Curated* — bloodless, overused, means nothing
- *Premium / luxury / investment piece / affordable luxury* — wrong category
- *Elevate / elevated / elevate your look* — corporate
- *Effortless* — ironic, because it takes effort to make something look effortless and saying it ruins the effect
- *Game-changer / must-have / staple / wardrobe essential* — bingo card
- *Drop* used as a verb on every announcement (use rarely, save it for actual drops)
- *Treat yourself* — patronizing
- *We're so excited / We're thrilled / Great news / Introducing* — broadcast openers; just say the thing
- *Leverage, synergy, ecosystem, journey, experience* — corporate dictionary
- *Unisex, gender-neutral* (as a value prop) — performative; just make things and let people wear them
- *Sustainable, conscious, mindful* — unless we have something specific and true to claim, don't reach for these as decoration
- ***Lose them / crush them / throw them around / wear them till they break*** — anything that frames the product as **disposable**. The frames are well-made and accessible — not cheap or throwaway. *"Buy another pair"* is fine; *"lose them, get another"* is not. The buy-multiples worldview is about **building a collection / changing moods**, not about being careless with the product. (See §5.10 for the full rule and *"How to apply"* notes.)

### 5.6 The Voice Gut-Check (run on every line)

Before any copy ships — product page, email, IG caption, packaging insert, error state — ask:

1. **Would I text this to a friend?** If it sounds like a brand announcement, rewrite it as a text and see what falls away.
2. **Is the customer the hero, or is Jaxy?** Lines about *us* ("we're so excited," "our new collection," "we craft") almost always fail. Rewrite to make the reader the subject.
3. **Could a competitor say this exact line?** If Quay, Le Specs, or Sunski could paste this onto their site without changing a word, it's not Jaxy. Add specificity until only Jaxy could've written it.

If a line passes all three, ship it.

### 5.7 Copy Principles

**1. Every product name is a lifestyle moment, not a description.**
- Bad: "JX1001-BLK" / "Round Sunglasses"
- Good: "Golden Hour" / "Sunday Drive" / "Main Character" / "Last Light"
- Names should make someone want to *be* the name.

**2. CTAs are invitations, not commands.**
- Bad: "Shop Now" / "Learn More" / "View All"
- Good: "Find Your Pair" / "Pick a Mood" / "Start Here" / "See the Lineup" / "Try a Different Vibe"

**3. Lead with the person, not the product.**
- Bad: "Polarized round frame with spring hinges"
- Good: "Made for the friend who's been wearing the same tortoise pair since 2019. (Hi, you.)"
- Tell us who it's for. The specs come second.

**4. Short sentences hit harder. Fragments hit harder still.**
- Bad: "Our collection features a wide variety of styles designed to complement every face shape."
- Good: "48 styles. Every shape. Starting at $24."
- Periods are rhythm. Use them.

**5. Lead with feeling. Follow with fact.**
- Bad: "UV400 protection with polarized lenses and spring hinges"
- Good: "Built to live in. UV400. Polarized options. Spring hinges so they bounce back when you sit on them."
- Feeling first, spec sheet second, never spec sheet only.

**6. Never explain what's obvious.**
- Bad: "Sunglasses protect your eyes from harmful UV rays while expressing your personal style."
- Good: *(skip it)*
- If everyone already knows, you sound like a press release.

**7. Be specific or be silent.**
- "Golden hour on the 10" beats "summer evenings"
- "The friend who says yes to dessert" beats "for fun lovers"
- "Texts you back in 30 seconds" beats "responsive"
- Specificity is the whole game.

### 5.9 Voice Do's

- Write the line as a text first, then edit it once for the page
- Talk to *one person* — never "everyone" or "customers"
- Use "you" and "your" — make it a conversation, not a broadcast
- Name specific things: *the 10 at sunset, Sunday morning, the friend who says yes, Spring 2026* — never "our values" or "our community"
- Use sentence fragments for rhythm. Trust the reader.
- Make the customer the hero. Always.
- When you do reference Jaxy, do it like a friend signing off — *"— Jaxy"* — not a corporate "we"

### 5.10 Voice Don'ts

- Don't say "luxury," "premium," "investment piece," or "affordable luxury"
- Don't say "curated"
- Don't open emails with "We're so excited" / "Great news" / "Introducing" — just say the thing
- Don't apologize for our price point. Don't perform humility about it either. Own it.
- Don't over-explain. If you need a paragraph, you need a friend to take it down to two lines.
- Don't broadcast. Talk to one person.
- Don't use exclamation marks in product copy. One per email, max. Social media gets more leeway.
- No emoji in product copy or site copy. Social and email get them, sparingly, with intent.
- Never use "Jaxy Eyewear" — just "Jaxy"
- Don't use *they/them* about your customer to be safe — use *you*. It's warmer and dodges the question.
- **Don't frame the product as disposable.** Never *"lose them," "crush them," "throw them around," "wear them till they break,"* or "you can afford to lose them." The frames are well-made and accessible — not cheap or throwaway. **Why:** language that sounds careless undercuts the value position. We sell at $25-30 retail with real design — that's *accessible*, not *disposable*. **How to apply:** the buy-multiples worldview is real and stays — but frame it as *building a collection* or *changing moods*, not *replacing what you broke*. Use *"come back for another pair," "different mood, different pair," "build the closet,"* etc.
- **Don't say "Made in LA" or "Made in California."** The frames are *designed* in LA but *manufactured* overseas. The truthful claim is **"Designed in LA"** — never *"Made in LA"* or any phrasing that implies the product is manufactured locally (e.g., *"crafted in LA," "built in LA," "from our LA workshop"*). **Why:** this is a brand-integrity rule, not a voice rule — false manufacturing claims expose the brand to legal risk and erode customer trust if discovered. **How to apply:** when origin shows up in copy, use *"Designed in LA," "Designed in California,"* or *"Born in LA"* (origin of the brand and design, not the product). See `STORE-COPY.md` "Material Claims — TRUE vs FALSE" for the full TRUE/FALSE table.

### 5.13 Product Naming Philosophy

Every Jaxy frame should have a **lifestyle name** — a word or phrase that evokes a feeling, a place, a moment, or an attitude. The name should make someone want to *be* the person who wears that frame.

**Naming Categories:**

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| LA / California moments | Golden Hour, Sunset Strip, Pacific, Canyon, Malibu, Mulholland, Last Light |
| Attitude / energy | Main Character, Easy Rider, Day One, No Filter, Soft Launch |
| Music / culture | Encore, B-Side, Opening Act, Slow Dance, Headliner, Sound Check |
| Timeless cool | Sunday Drive, Café Run, Rooftop, Getaway, Long Weekend |

**Naming Rules:**
- 1-3 words max
- No generic descriptors (not "Classic Round" or "Modern Square")
- Should work as a hashtag (#MainCharacter, #GoldenHour)
- Should sound right when someone says "I'm wearing the [name]"
- Should pass the *"would I name a playlist this?"* test

### 5.15 Voice References — Who We Steal From, and What

The voice should sound like Jaxy, not like a remix. But three brands punch above their weight on copy and naming, and each one teaches a different lesson. When stuck, study these.

#### Crap Eyewear ([crapeyewear.com](https://www.crapeyewear.com))
**Lesson: own the joke. Make the brand a single sentence.**

- **Tagline:** *"Don't Worry, Be Crappy."* — owns their "low-end" name, turns it into swagger, doubles as a Bobby McFerrin reference. Whole brand voice fits in four words.
- **Origin-as-voice:** Founders Peter Nussbaum and Nick Potash say *"We were punk-ass kids and thought the market was so boring."* That sentence IS the brand. Their About page doesn't sound like a company describing itself — it sounds like the founders explaining why they had to do this.
- **Naming system:** *The [Noun]* structure, every time. Cultural references baked in: *The TV Eye* (Iggy Pop), *The Nudie Mag* (Adam Sandler bit), *The Bikini Vision*, *The Paradise Machine*, *The Suntan Underground*, *The Chaos Vault*. The names create a *world* — surf, skate, punk, late-70s LA. You wear a Crap and you're part of that world.
- **Geographic specificity:** Venice, not "LA." Beachside. Tested by skaters and surfers who actually live there. The map pin is part of the voice.

**For Jaxy:**
- Our origin story (*"we kept walking out of stores annoyed"*) is the same DNA — punch it up. Make the About page sound like the founder talking, not the company describing itself.
- Get more geographically specific where it lands. *Echo Park. Silver Lake. Eastside. The 10 at sunset.* Specificity beats "LA" every time.
- Don't try to copy *"Don't Worry, Be Crappy."* Do find Jaxy's equivalent: a single phrase that carries the whole brand. (See 5.16 below.)

#### Meller ([mellerbrand.com](https://mellerbrand.com))
**Lesson: a naming system is a worldview.**

- **Naming system:** Every frame named after an African place — *Nyasa, Malabo, Kribi, Kubu, Chauen, Yster, Suku.* That's not random, that's a thesis: a Barcelona brand celebrating African geography as global beauty. The system *is* the brand position.
- **Photography/voice match:** Bold visuals, funny captions, low brand-volume. The product does most of the talking.
- **Pricing as confidence:** $40-60 with no price-apologizing copy. They don't say "luxury" and they don't say "affordable." They just price it and move on.

**For Jaxy:**
- Our naming system is *lifestyle moments* — that's good, but commit harder. Every frame name should land in one of the four categories (LA/CA moments, attitude, music/culture, timeless cool). If a name doesn't fit a category, it doesn't ship.
- Don't apologize for the price and don't perform humility about it. Just say *$28* and let it land.

#### Quay ([quay.com](https://www.quay.com))
**Lesson: punctuation, rhythm, and CTAs do the personality work.**

- **Naming system:** Pop-culture lifestyle phrases — *Hit Single, Born Ready, Level Up, Talk Nice, Say Less, On a Whim.* Everyday slang, used as frame names. The names are texts.
- **CTA voice:** *"SHOP THE VIBE."* Not "Shop." Not "Shop the collection." Every button has a small piece of personality.
- **Page architecture as voice:** A whole "New to Quay" page that feels like a friend showing you around. Not a footer link — a destination.

**For Jaxy:**
- Steal the CTA philosophy without copying the copy. Every button should sound like Jaxy. *"Find Your Pair," "Pick a Mood," "See the Lineup."* Never *"Shop Now."*
- Build a "New Here?" / first-visitor page that does what Quay does — face-shape guide, the case for owning more than one, founder note, first-purchase code. Make it feel like a friend's apartment, not a homepage.

### 5.16 The Voice Anchor (locked)

**The anchor: *"More frames. Less shame."***

This is the single phrase that carries the brand. It owns the buy-a-lot position with a wink, has rhythm, and is something competitors can't easily copy. It does the same job for Jaxy that *"Don't Worry, Be Crappy"* does for Crap.

**The anchor lives in (every one of these, every time):**
- IG bio
- Inside of every case
- Back of every hangtag
- Footer of every email
- Bottom of receipts and order confirmations
- Footer block of the website
- Wholesale decks (back cover)

**The anchor does NOT replace the tagline family.** Each other phrase has a specific job (see Section 1, Tagline Family). The anchor is the constant. The family flexes by context.

**Tagline Family — the assigned jobs:**

| Phrase | Job |
|---|---|
| **More frames. Less shame.** | **Anchor.** Everywhere, every time. |
| Look like yourself on purpose. | Brand thesis — About page opener, hero campaigns, manifesto moments. |
| Made for changing your mind. | Hero / collection / campaign headline (rotates). |
| Two of these, please. | Email subject lines, packaging notes, social — the buy-a-pair call-to-feeling. |
| Glasses for everyone we know. | About page closer, wholesale decks, community / family moments. |
| Wear them. Live in them. | Legacy — already on packaging and key visuals. Keep where it lives, but stop deploying as primary. |
| Born in LA. Built for everywhere. | Origin moments, About page, founder notes. |
| Bold frames. Real prices. | Wholesale and B2B — pragmatic moments. |

Repetition is how a phrase becomes an anchor. *"Just Do It"* worked because Nike said it ten thousand times. Now we say *"More frames. Less shame."* everywhere.
